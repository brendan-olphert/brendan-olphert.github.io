Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Problem Solving Experience

I became suprisingly blocked on a very simple problem in the reflections for this week, it presented a simple if/else and asked that I write a function that would print pass.

Firstly I tried explaining to my housemate what I was trying to do and what I thought I needed to do, basically using him as a rubber duck. I then wrote out what the function would need to do on paper and began researching/remembering the commands I would need. I set up a test space (REPL.IT) to work in and tried it out.

It didnt work, The function I had been provided was simple and should produce a pass with what i had sent it. I checked my code and still found the commands I used what I thought I should use. I became frustrated at this point and discussed it with a peer from the course, we compared experience and found we had both done more than we needed to.

Basically I had gotten so focused on one part of the function that I thought would require a fix that i hadnt stepped back and seen the rather simple solution in front of me of just using the funtion I was presented with.

I learned I need to stop getting ahead of myself and look at the problem for what it is and not how difficult I think its going to be.

Problem Solving Techniques

Pseudocode

I am fairly confident with Pseudocode, I have been breaking down blocking out what I need to do on paper alot anyway.

Trying something

I love getting through workshops and trying things out, getting to a level where you understand the concepts enough to learn by trial and error is something I really enjoy.

Rubber ducky method

I really like this idea, I feel like im going to use my cat instead though, but i think its a great first step before pseudocode.

Reading error messages

I find these hugely helpful and they have gotten me through alot of the kata challenges this week, it's kind of my go to.

Console.logging

I have not actually done heaps of this but I am starting to, so its probably to process im least confident but can see myself using it alot more in sprint five.

Googling

Been doint alot of this lately!,and im confident in how to research what i'm after, but the foundations sprints have left me with some pretty excellent resourses I have bookmarked also.

Asking your peers and coaches for help

Always a must! Its important to have a go and review your work but the people around you are an incredible resource and I have had plenty of help.
