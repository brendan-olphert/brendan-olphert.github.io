Identity, Values and Strengths

My core personal values are empathy, compassion loyalty and integrity and as a person a try to adhere strongly to these values; Empathy is vital for working with others as its what really allows us to hear and understand others, coupled with compassion and respect its what allows us to reconcile our ideas with others. I’m often described as a very loyal person, but I take having made commitments seriously, emotional commitments to family and friends most of all. Integrity is perhaps the most important, as core values can mean very little without the motivation and conviction to follow them at all times. The Briggs-Meyers personality test classifies me as introverted, intuitive, feeling and perceiving, and I largely agree; “INFPs are imaginative idealists, guided by their own core values and beliefs. To a Healer, possibilities are paramount; the realism of the moment is only of passing concern. They see potential for a better future, and pursue truth and meaning with their own individual flair. INFPs are sensitive, caring, and compassionate, and are deeply concerned with the personal growth of themselves and others. Individualistic and nonjudgmental, INFPs believe that each person must find their own path.”
