Workload Management

I intend on being very habitual in my approach to this course. At the start of each sprint I will read through what is required and break down the weeks workload into practical tasks, I picked up a habit of using task management software Trello in my last job and have been making up a trello board for each sprint at the start of the week. while this takes some time I find it helps me engage with and understand what is expected of me and what I can expect it terms of managing my workload. I have also started utilising timekeeping software to manage breaks and I set small goals of what units I should have completed on certain days at the start of each week.

Wellbeing and Support

I try to be very aware of my limitations and know myself well in this sense, as such I acknowledge my limits and seek help and support.

A big part of me relocating to Auckland to undertake this course was that my family and friends are in this city, I thought it best to have a support network around me while undertaking this course. My old friend and housemate is also currently completing foundations and I am finding havinf someone to relate to and reflect on the course with invaluable.

Scheduling

I fortunately have no other major commitments while undertaking the course, I was working weekends and had planned on doing so throughout foundations but they are very late night shifts, so while I have the time to work the shifts I am finding the effect the 4am finishes have on my sleep cylcles for the next few days unsustainable. as such I no longer plan on working no more than one shift a week and only if I can work an earlier shift. i will also be finishing up there entirely sooner than expected. Having just recently moved here I also have very little social commitments.

I intend on keeping sundays blocked out to see my family and friends but I still like to look over my (hopefully finished) work on sunday evenings.I plan on structuring my week every monday and set personal deadlines and goals throughout the week.

I have largely worked evenings in life so im very used to getting alot of work done late into the evening, I wish to focus on what 'kind' of work I do and when. I am already finding mornings a much better time for me to deal with reflections and self assment and the nights far more suited to workshops and practical coding.
