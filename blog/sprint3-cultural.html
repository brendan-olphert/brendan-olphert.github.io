Emotional Intelligence

EQ vs. IQ

IQ (intelligence quotient) refers to skills and traits valued by traditional standardized intelligence testing and his often equated with high achievement in academic fields.

Traits associated with IQ include:

  • Quantative reasoning
  • Visual and spacial processing
  • Short-term memory
  • Language comprehension
  • Fluid reasoning

EQ (emotional Intelligence) refers to a person's ability to perceive, control, evaluate, and express emotions, it is the skills and ability to recognise and manage your own emotions and the emotions of others.

Traits associated with EQ include:

  • Evaluating how others feel
  • Relating to others
  • Facilitating Social Communication
  • Identifying emotions
  • Perceptive to others feelings

How is EQ relevant?

Individuals with high EQ can manage emotions, use their emotions to facilitate thinking, understand emotional meanings and accurately perceive others' emotions. Through practices such as journaling, attention training and concious listening we can develop skillsets to improve our emotional intelligence

The abilities of individuals with high emotional intelligence are widely recognised as desireble in workplace and educational settings.

In the workplace individuals with developeddd emotional intelligence are viewed as being skilled with:

  • Collaboration and Teamwork
  • Initiatve
  • Self-confidence
  • Relating to others
  • Communication
